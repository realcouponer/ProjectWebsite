System Modules

CO-CREATING THE ENVIRONMENT THAT ENABLES FINANCIAL LIFE

The Superalgos System is being co-built by a group of development teams distributed around the world. It is a collaborative effort by which teams come together, each taking full responsibility over a specific part of the system.

The system is subdivided in over 40 modules, thus, only a few teams contributing early on will be able to participate in this role. In order to achieve maximum resilience, teams develop a few modules each. You and your team are welcome to join in.

Development teams' work in the project is characterized as voluntary work. In order to incentivize contributions, the project allocates 15% of the total supply of ALGO tokens to System Development.

A fraction of that percentage is reserved for each module, so as to persuade teams to assume full responsibility over its development and long-term maintenance. The precise allocation depends on the module's category, which is a function of the level of criticality, urgency and difficulty. There are five categories, each with a bounty, as shown below.

In terms of incentives, the category number indicates the amount of ALGO tokens to be allocated to teams undertaking the development and maintenance of a specific module, expressed in millions (i.e. a category 5 module allocates 5 million tokens).

Maintenance & Improvements

Assuming full responsibility over the module means teams developing modules are also tasked with their long-term maintenance. As such, teams are expected to get truly close to the project, becoming a fundamental part of the project's success.

While we foresee a period of time after development in which modules stabilize, the truth is that the project is in constant evolution. Any new piece of functionality or requirement falling within the scope of a particular module is the responsibility of the team incharge of the module.

Mining ALGO Tokens

Volunteering your work and developing a system module leads to the community granting you access to mining ALGO tokens from your module's pool, and also to collect a fee from every financial being living in the platform once the service is deployed and open to the public.

Miners are Ethereum smart contracts programmed to allocate ALGO tokens from a particular pool to a specific set of beneficiary addresses.

They have a pre-defined mining rate and a daily transaction interval. Once deployed, miners deliver 50% of the remaining tokens in the pool each year. Put in other words, there is a halving event in the mining rate at each year mark counting from the activation date; i.e. 50% of the total amount of tokens in a pool are mined during the first year, 25% during the second year, 12.5% during the third and so on, until year ten in which all remaining tokens in the specific pool are mined—see the table below for clarity.

The number of tokens reserved for each module is related to the module's category. There are five categories, from one to five: the category number indicates the amount of ALGO tokens in the pool expressed in millions (i.e. a category 5 module has 5 million tokens).

The category number is a reflection of the perceived level of criticality, urgency and difficulty entailed in the particular module. The higher the number, the more critical, urgent and / or difficult the area of responsibility is recognized to be.

Acquiring a Mining Agent

In order to acquire a miner, new teams need to show the community that they are qualified for the job. To do that, they apply for a specific module and start developing it. After some time, the team will have produced a first usable version of the module, and gone through the integration process so as to make it usable by the community.

If the work delivered satisfies the community, a proposal to assign a miner to the new team is raised and voted, and the miner is activated.

Example

Let's use an example to bring everything said so far down to earth.

A team of three developers wishes to contribute to the development of the Superalgos System. They get in touch with the project, meet the technical core team maintaining the AdvancedAlgos organization in Github, discuss priorities and apply to work on a specific System Module.

The selected module becomes their area of responsibility. Every piece of functionality identified (or to be identified) that logically falls within the scope of that particular module becomes the team's responsibility. Their mission is to develop the module, refine it and maintain it.

The team starts out by joining regular developer meetings; they discuss their module with other developers, getting acquainted with the system architecture and gathering initial definitions. They agree with the community on the scope of a first usable version of the module and start programming.

While developing version one of the module, they stay in touch with other developers and the technical core team, consulting on any issues that may arise and showcasing their progress at developer meetings.

Eventually, they start integrating the module into the overall system, polishing the rough edges until the functionality becomes usable.

By that time, the rest of developers have become acquainted with the new team, having interacted frequently and followed their work closely. The constant revisions through regular meetings help the new team stay on course, remain focused in priorities and programing following agreed best practices.

This initial piece of work is enough to show the rest of developers that the new team is a worthy acquisition. Without further due and through an express voting session, the new team is granted the corresponding miner, which is readily activated.

The new team is now a key part of the community. It keeps developing their module until the last bit of functionality is integrated. People report bugs until the module stabilizes and requires less and less work. The team stays alert, updating the module every time a new requirement arises.

Exit

If at any point in time an individual contributor wishes to step aside, or fails to deliver as per the expectations of the rest of System Development Teams, the project will help the person make a clean exit by delegating responsibilities into:

  • other members of the same team;

  • other teams.

In the first case, the team's relationship with the rest of the ecosystem remains the same and the team shall keep all of its responsibilities and entitlements through an internal change in management.

In the second case, the departing team will keep all the ALGO it may have been allocated up to that point in time.

However, the departing team will resign entitlements to future module fees and to minning whatever part of the pool that may be pending.
