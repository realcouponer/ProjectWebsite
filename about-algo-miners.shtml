• ALGO Miners... the backbone of the Superalgos incentives scheme

Proof of Worth

Mining the ALGO token has nothing to do with hardware rigs, electricity or proof of work. Instead, it is about investing time and effort to add value to the project.


Bitcoin borrowed the mining analogy from the mining industry as a way to convey the notion that securing the network requires significant investments of various resources, starting with the initial cost of the mining rig and infrastructure along with ongoing operational costs.

Entities willing to make such investments and provide such valuable service to the network would in turn be compensated with newly minted bitcoin.

It is in that same spirit that we too borrow the term. We use the term mining to describe how any person or company may help secure the success of the Superalgos project, and obtain ALGO tokens in reward.

Like with bitcoin, mining ALGO Tokens requires an upfront investment, in our case, mostly of time and effort, along with an ongoing commitment of similar resources in the long run.

Beyond being the medium of exchange within the ecosystem and a form of energy enabling financial life, ALGO is also the backbone of the Superalgos project incentives scheme. People and companies working with the project, starting with the Core Team, the Dev Team and the larger Superalgos Team as a whole are all incentivized with ALGO, for as long as they keep adding value to the project.

ALGO Miners

An ALGO Miner is an Ethereum smart contract, and the instrument used to distribute ALGO among all the entities that add value to the Superalgos project. Mining ALGO requires zero technical skills as the whole process is managed by the Core Team.


The Superalgos incentives scheme is designed to satisfy a fundamental requirement: keep key stakeholders incentivized throughout the life of the project, meaning both in the short term and in the long run.

For short term incentives, the project counts with a limited number of tokens reserved for Software Development and Project Development. Added together, that reserve amounts to 25% of the total supply of tokens.

For long term incentives, we count with an unlimited source of tokens: a fee all financial beings will need to pay in order to run on the platform. This feature is not yet implemented, but will be in the future.

As such, ALGO miners have three main features:

  • allocate a specific number of tokens from the corresponding pool (software or project development) to a beneficiary address;

  • route a fraction of the fee financial beings will pay to a beneficiary address;

  • participate in the governance of the project (this feature is not implemented nor even defined yet).

In essense, entities holding an ALGO Miner become co-owners of the Superalgos project.

Short Term Incentives


There are 5 categories of miners: from Category 1 to Category 5.

The category number indicates the number of ALGO tokens—in millions—that the miner is programmed to allocate (total capacity) along a period of time.

For instance, a Category 1 Miner has the capacity to allocate 1 million tokens, while a Category 5 Miner allocates 5 million tokens.

Miners have a predefined mining rate and a daily transaction interval. This means that a miner allocates a certain number of ALGO to the beneficiary address once per day.

Miners allocate ALGO as follows:

  • one half (½ or 50%) of the total capacity is allocated on the first year;

  • one fourth (¼ or 25%) of the total capacity is allocated on the second year;

  • one eighth (⅛ or 12.5%) is allocated on the third year... and so it goes, halving each year for a total of nine years;

  • on year 10, all of the remaining ALGO are allocated.

Put in other words, miners deliver 50% of the remaining tokens each year. Please see the tables below for clarity.

Long Term Incentives


In the not-so-distant future, algorithms in the platform will need to pay a fee to stay alive and running.

Part of the fee will go to cover expenses directly related to running the algorithm, for example, cloud resources such as memory, processing capacity, storage space and bandwidth.

Another part of the fee will go to cover indirect expenses, such as running the rest of the software infrastructure.

Yet another fraction of the fee will go to paying for the work a number of people and companies do in many different domains in order for the project to thrive. Such work includes software maintenance, marketing or management to name a few. This is the fraction of the fee that is distributed through ALGO Miners.

The fraction of the fee corresponding to ALGO Miners will of course be distributed in proportion to the category of each miner, and will be paid for as long as the miner is active.
