Overview

The charts interface is an HTML canvas featuring three overlaid visual spaces working simultaneously:

  • Charts timeline: It is a timeline in which indicators and sensors' productsIndicators and sensors' mission is creating products that others can consume. To do this, they run processes which produce and store datasets.

  • Floating notifications: It is a layer of information displayed on top of the timeline, typically containing algobots' related information such as bots' runtime position and messages.

  • Control & information panels: There are two types of panels. One set of panels is built in the interface and show certain control elements to dictate what exactly is shown on the timeline (list of bot products, for instance). A second type of panels is created by plotters to display additional information for certain products.

  5. This horizontal space between the charts and the bottom bar is used to control chart breakpoints when running on backtest mode. Click on the space to place a breakpoint and click on a breakpoint to remove it. Chart breakpoints are used to visually debug the trading strategy, pausing execution when the corresponding candle becomes active.

    In order for chart breakpoints to actually pause execution at the specific point in time in which it was placed, you first need to place a breakpoint in the following line of the User.Bot.js code:

    
if (global.AT_BREAKPOINT === true) {
      if (FULL_LOG === true) { logger.write(MODULE_NAME, "[INFO] run -> loop -> Plot Breakpoint Hit."); } // Place breakpoint in this line
}

    Upon execution, a chart breakpoint pauses execution and takes the user to that specific line of code, provided that a breakpoint was placed at that line in the first place.

Navigating the Charts Timeline

With your mouse:

  • Zoom in and out by turning the wheel forwards and blackguards respectively. Notice how the time scale is automatically adjusted for different levels of zoom. The timescale is represented by the actual candles and the time value of each candle is indicated in the time information panel.

  • Click and drag to pan across the timeline.

Products Panel

Use the products panel to turn layers ON and OFF –including candlesticks, volumes, indicators and algobots– by clicking on the bars in the center. A green bar means the layer is showing in the charts. A yellow bar means the layer is being calculated and made available to dependent layers. A red bar means the layer is not calculated, and not showing.

To scroll the panel simply place your mouse pointer on top of it and turn the scroll wheel.

Trading Bots in Action

Algobots produce three different datasets constituting three different products:

  • Live trading history

  • Backtest trading history

  • Competition trading history

When either of the trading products is on in the Products Panel, the Plotters-Trading associated with trading bots renders a number of visual clues in the timeline to indicate various bot actions. Notice the small triangles in the figure below:

  • A triangle pointing downwards represents a sell order.

    • When the triangle is transparent with a red outline, it represents the moment in time and price point in which the order was placed.

    • When the triangle is solid red, it represents the moment in time and price point in which the algobot got a confirmation that the order was filled.

  • Similarly, a triangle pointing upwards with a green outline means a buy order was placed and the solid green triangle indicates that the order was filled.

  • Notice that outlined and solid triangles may overlap if the zoom level does not allow rendering each triangle in separate candles. In that case, it means that the order was placed and filled during the period of time represented by the candle.
