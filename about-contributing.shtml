Co-leading the Superalgos Project

JOIN THE SUPERMIND!

Superalgos is a collective project, open for people to join and contribute. As a community, we aim to attract the best minds and spirits; people who share our vision and values; women and men willing to drive change and pursue the project's long-term goals.

At this early stage, people joining the project are those willing to undertake a leadership role: our work organization is founded on the distribution of responsibilities.

As leaders, we are expected to assume full responsibility on our specific fields of work. This means our scope of work is doing whatever it takes for our area of responsibility to be developed to the level the project needs at each point in time. In fact, the extent of the work of leaders is dynamic. As the project evolves, new needs arise.

Leaders are flexible and constantly adapt, welcoming new requirements, iterating on solutions and even redoing work as many times as may be necessary. Our mission is to evolve our work until it reaches the expected level of quality or efficiency.

We work in domains (e.g. Marketing, Business, Software Development, etc) interacting with peers that collaborate in a horizontal and organic fashion. There are no formal supervisors or hard hierarchies. Instead, we all follow up with each other, coordinating intersecting issues, planning integration and setting up collective goals in order to align efforts.

Decisions are made by consensus within each domain and with the larger community. Ultimately, each individual person's views, choices and decisions are influenced by their peers. After all, we aim to satisfy the needs of the project as perceived by the wider community.

Leaders are open-minded people who welcome diversity of opinion. They are always willing to negotiate when confronted with diverging views, reach agreements quickly and resolve disputes amicably.

Leaders may be individuals or teams of any number of people sharing one specific area of responsibility.

Open Positions & Incentives

WE VALUE YOUR CONTRIBUTION

Our work in the Superalgos Project is characterized as volunteer work. In order to incentivize contributions, the project reserves 10% of the total supply of ALGO tokens to be distributed among the leadership of the project.

Areas of responsibility are categorized in numbers, from 1 to 5. The category number is a reflection of the perceived level of criticality, urgency or scope entailed in the particular area of responsibility. The higher the number, the more critical, urgent or the largest the scope the area of responsibility is recognized to have.

In terms of incentives, the category number indicates the amount of ALGO tokens to be allocated to leaders undertaking a specific area of responsibility, expressed in millions (i.e. a category 5 area allocates 5 million tokens).

Domains and specific areas of responsibility are dynamically identified and assigned by the community as the project advances.

Incentives Distribution

UNDERTAKING AN AREA OF RESPONSIBILITY

In order to be granted the assignment of an area of responsibility, leaders need to show the community that they are the right person or team for the job. To do that, they apply for a specific area and start working. After some time, they will have produced a body of work consisting of a usable first version of whatever their area of responsibility requires. Once the piece of work satisfies the community, the area of responsibility is granted and the new leader acquires entitlements to the corresponding incentives in the form of ALGO tokens.

After leaders are confirmed in their area of responsibility, a smart contract starts delivering ALGO tokens from the corresponding pool to the leaders' ALGO wallet.

The project expects leaders to honor their responsibilities for the long run and aims to align all stakeholders interests via a smart incentives scheme. In that light, ALGO tokens in the pool are delivered in monthly installments under the following rule: 50% of the remaining tokens in the pool are delivered each year. Put in other words, there is a halving event in the delivery rate at each year mark counting from the date the area of responsibility was assigned; i.e. 50% of the total amount of tokens in the pool are delivered during the first year, 25% during the second year, 12.5% during the third and so on, until the tenth year in which all remaining tokens are delivered—see the table below for clarity.

Additional Incentives

THINKING LONG TERM

In addition to the incentives in the pool of tokens corresponding to each area of responsibility, leaders are also entitled to a second form of incentives that shall become available once the service is launched to the general public: leaders will collect fees from all financial beings living in the platform.

There are no precise definitions as of how fees will work at this point in time. The intent, however, is clear: to complement and extend incentives so that leaders keep up the good work for the long run.

Exit

THE WAY OUT

If at any point in time an individual leader wishes to step aside, or fails to deliver as per the expectations of the rest of the community, the project will help the person make a clean exit by delegating responsibilities into:

  • other members of the same team;

  • other teams.

In the first case, the team's relationship with the rest of the community remains the same and the team shall keep all of its responsibilities and entitlements through an internal change in management.

In the second case, the departing team will keep all the ALGO it may have been allocated up to that point in time. However, it will resign entitlements to future token deliveries and to future fees collected from financial beings.

Example

HOW IT ALL WORKS

Let's use an example to bring everything said so far down to earth.

A team of three creatives wishes to take on the area of Youtube Tutorials Channel. They get in touch with the project, meet a few teams in the Marketing domain and also meet technical teams developing the system. They discuss views, priorities and agree on which tutorial to do first.

The selected tutorial will constitute the body of work the new team will present to the community to prove they are the right team for the job.

The team starts out by joining regular marketing meetings; they discuss their tutorial, expected format, look & feel, communication strategy and so on. They also meet developers and get acquainted with the system architecture and gather the definitions they might need. They do their own homework, use the system, learn it, ask around and read any available documentation.

While producing the tutorial, they stay in touch with other teams in Marketing as well as with developers, consulting on any issues that may arise and showcasing their progress to get early feedback.

Eventually, the team wraps up the tutorial and presents a fully-working version to the community.

By that time, a part of the community has become acquainted with the new team, having interacted frequently and followed their work closely. The constant revisions through regular meetings helped the new team stay on course, remain focused in priorities and producing the tutorial following agreed best practices.

This initial piece of work along with the human interactions experienced along the way are enough to show the community that the new team is a worthy acquisition. Without further due and through an express voting session, the new team is granted the corresponding area of responsibility, and 30 days later the team starts getting ALGO tokens in their ALGO wallet.

The new team is now a key part of the community. It keeps producing tutorials, promoting the Tutorials Youtube Channel and integrating their material with the rest of the marketing mix.

Along the way, the system evolves and some tutorials need adjustments or become obsolete. The team is fully responsible for staying on top of the system's progress in order to keep their material up to date.

By the second year the team has got 75% of the tokens in their area's pool and are collecting fees from the moment the service launched to the public.
