Open Source, Ownership & Governance

Is Superalgos an open source project?

Yes. Superalgos is a collaborative project open to all people.

Our code is available at GitHub for everyone to verify and audit. It is also available for the to fork and innovate in a permissionless manner within the .

The project aims to decentralize the development and maintenance of the system into a worldwide network of development teams that may work in coordination with the Core Team or in an independent manner. In a similar fashion, the project welcomes contributions from people in fields such as collective intelligence, social psychology, game theory, artificial intelligence, governance, management, economy, communication, marketing, law and business.

All of this work is incentivized with the project's native crypto token, the . In addition, ALGO is also used to incentivize to participate in , awarding ALGO as prize money. Finally, the ALGO Token will be the medium of payment will use to pay in the , and the channel to transfer the corresponding fees to Algo-Makers, who own the algo-rental business.

The has a strong open source vocation and a commitment to a fully transparent and auditable operation.

In order to protect the people investing substantial time and effort building the ALGO Ecosystem, the project's software license features a restriction by which permissionless innovation is allowed only within the ALGO Token ecosystem.

People and companies are granted rights to access source code, produce derived works, freely redistribute, use, modify and run the software for personal or commercial use, always making use of the ALGO Token as the sole medium of exchange, and within the Superalgos Network, while making all distributions available under the same license.

Who owns the Superalgos Project?

Superalgos is a collective project owned collectively by the group of stakeholders adding value to the endeavor. At this early stage, the main stakeholders are:

  • The Community; formed by a heterogeneous group of people counting mainly developers, traders and analysts, self-organizing in teams, which form organically through spontaneous relationships.
  • Team Members; that is the group of people working in the following : Development, Business, Marketing & Management. These two pages explain how to join the Development Team and how to join the Business, Marketing and Management Teams.

As the project advances, more stakeholders will get involved, as described in the Ecosystem page.

How is the Superalgos Project governed?

During the early stages of the project, Superalgos is governed by the , to this day, consisting of the group of co-founders. The Core Team is an open horizontal organization in which decisions are made democratically through a typical open source meritocracy: the more value a person adds, the more people listens to her and the more they accompany her proposals.

A number of people work on other Superalgos Teams, in the following domains: Development, Business, Marketing & Management. We call these people , and the Core Team is—actually—a sub set of the Team Members group. Team Members may be invited to the Core Team after showing exceptional commitment to the project and a particular talent for leadership.

These two pages explain how to join the Development Team and how to join the Business, Marketing and Management Teams.

As the project develops further and forms of decentralized governance are tried and tested in other projects, Superalgos intends to implement some form of decentralized governance system. Projects in the decentralization space working on governance solutions are welcome to get in touch with us.

Do I need to open up my Algorithms to participate?

No.

Superalgos does not expect people to bring their and make them open source to participate in our or the .

Instead, we encourage people to come together and build Algorithms in a cooperative fashion, most of the times starting off with Algorithms that already exist and are openly shared within the for everyone to be able to improve on.

It actually is the other way round. You are not supposed to come in and share your Algorithms. The way it works is that you come in, and we share our algos with you. It is up to you if you wish to become a valuable member of the Community and start contributing your knowledge to improve on the Community's collective wisdom, or not.

Furthermore, the is designed to remove trust requirements and to allow entities to run their own , which enables running Algorithms without disclosing their source code or having to share them with anyone.

Every time a Competition finishes, the crowd forks the best algos to try to improve them. Also, anyone at any point in time can choose to fork any bot that has participated in a Competition, and whose code has been opened.

The platform's genealogy / ancestry system will track forking events as they happen, and establish the lineage of each bot, including details on each of the Teams that participated in training the Algorithm.

At this point in time this functionality is only at the analysis stage, thus will become available later on, as the platform evolves.

For Algorithms developed in times prior to the implementation of the genealogy features, we shall consider all active teams to be owners of all algorithms created.
