• Superalgos


    We invented a framework to disrupt
    and outperform the algorithmic trading model
    and are advancing on its implementation.

  • The Status Quo

    No one shares their algorithms
    or know-how.

    • Investment companies keep trading technology in total secrecy.
    • The first assumption is this gives them an edge against other market participants.
    • The second assumption is that algorithms lose performance if used broadly.
    • Even within a company, specialists work alone or in small groups.
    • Evolution is therefore slow and costly.

  • Our Approach

    Break the silos.
    This is how...

    • A global crowd creates trading algorithms.
    • Algorithms participate in competitions for prize money.
    • After each competition ends, algorithms are open-sourced within the community.
    • The crowd improves the best algorithms and put them to compete again.

  • How Science Works

    Scientific knowledge grows exponentially
    because of the publishing of discoveries and information spread.

    • Scientists freely take from other scientists' advances and keep building on them.
    • The limitless sharing capacity of the Internet has led to exponential scientific growth.
    • What if scientists worked in silos? We'd still be in the Stone Age!

  • Trading Intelligence Marketplace

    As intelligence grows,
    algorithms are put at the service of all people.

    • Investors of all calibers may rent the crowd's trading intelligence to manage their assets.
    • Algo-makers are paid for the use of their algos.
    • Algo rentals add incentives for algo-makers, on top of fun comps and prize money.
    • Constant evolution guarantees prompt replacement of algos that lose their edge.

  • Go To Market:
    World Championship

    A multi-layer league system
    designed to drive evolution towards trading big capitals.

    • Teams need to win minor leagues to move to higher leagues.
    • Bottom league runs short competitions, with minimal capital req. and small prizes.
    • Higher leagues use longer comps, more capital and offer significant prize money.
    • Teams winning a league get prize money to cover the capital req. for the next league.

  • Go To Market:
    Sponsors

    Algorithmic trading competitions,
    a win-win partnership with exchanges.

    • Exchanges host competitions to drive trading volume to their own businesses.
    • They sponsor significant prize money to incentivize algo-maker teams to compete.
    • Price money comes at no cost to exchanges as it fits within standard volume discounts.
    • Competitions become a customer acquisition strategy for exchanges.

  • The Main Challenge

    Bootstrapping the project
    until it snowballs!

    • The project is built on two parallel positive feedback loops that accelerate growth.
    • Both loops incentivize algo-makers, the key to an ever-growing crowd.

    More algo-makers → more competitors → more volume → more prize money → more algo-makers!

    More algo-makers → more intelligence → more performance → more demand for rentals → more algo-makers!

  • Competitors

    Even those with a crowd work in silos.

    • Quantopian, QuantConnect and Numerai outsource predictions / algos from crowds.
    • None harness the collective intelligence of their crowds through collaboration.

  • Business Model

    A volume flow business,
    negotiating cash-back discounts on trading fees with exchanges.

    • Scenario: consolidation in crypto, 100K rentals, €50K daily volume each.
    • Assumptions: exchanges fees ~ 0.1% (AVG maker/taker), volume discounts ~ 50%.
    • Daily totals:
      • €5 billion volume (~20% of current total daily crypto volume)
      • €5 million in exchanges fees (exchanges revenue)
      • €2.5 million in cash-back payments (daily company revenue)
    • Annualized totals:
      • €1,825 billion volume
      • 1.8 billion in exchanges fees (exchanges revenue)
      • €912 million in cash-back payments (yearly company revenue)
