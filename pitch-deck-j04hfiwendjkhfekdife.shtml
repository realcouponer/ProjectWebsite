  • The Status Quo

    No One Shares their Algorithms
    or Know-How

    • Investment companies keep trading technology in total secrecy.
    • The first assumption is this gives them an edge against other market participants.
    • The second assumption is that algorithms lose performance if used broadly.
    • Even within a company, specialists work alone or in small groups.
    • Evolution is therefore slow and costly.

  • Our Approach

    Break the Silos.
    This is how...

    • A global crowd creates trading algorithms.
    • Algorithms participate in competitions for prize money.
    • After each competition ends, algorithms are open sourced within the community.
    • The crowd improves the best algorithms and put them to compete again.

  • How Science Works

    Scientific Knowledge Grows Exponentially
    Because Discoveries are Published and Information Spread

    • Scientists freely take from other scientists' advances and keep building on them.
    • The limitless sharing capacity of the Internet has led to exponential scientific growth.
    • What if scientists worked in silos? We'd still be in the Stone Age!

  • Go To Market: World Cup

    A Multi-Layer League System
    Designed to Drive Evolution Towards Trading Big Capitals

    • Teams need to win minor leagues to move to higher leagues.
    • Bottom league runs short competitions, with minimal capital req. and small prizes.
    • Higher leagues use longer comps, more capital and offer significant prize money.
    • Teams winning a league get prize money to cover the capital req. for the next league.

  • Go To Market: Sponsors

    Algorithmic Trading Competitions,
    a Win-Win Partnership with Exchanges

    • Exchanges host competitions to drive trading volume to their own businesses.

    • Price money comes at no cost to exchanges as it fits within standard volume discounts.
    • Competitions become a customer acquisition strategy for exchanges.
