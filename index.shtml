The Status Quo

Trading Intelligence Evolves in Silos

Investment companies keep trading technology in absolut secrecy. The assumption is this gives them an edge over other market participants. It is also believed that algorithms lose performance if used too broadly. Evolution is therefore slow and costly.

Learn More

We believe we have identified a systemic weakness in the reigning active asset management model; a weakness that may explain why no trading intelligence developed within investment firms has ever evolved to the point of dominating the space or achieving a remarkably superior performance in the markets.

Companies acquire significant talent in the form of data analysts and tech resources; however, in our view, they may be failing at organizing human and machine resources in a way that would yield the best possible results.

Sometimes due to incentives structures, workflows, organizational requirements or segmentation of incumbencies—save for a few exceptions—there is little collaboration within firms.

In fact, small teams and sometimes even individuals work in absolute secrecy.

Needless to say, there certainly is zero cooperation in between firms.

Investment firms work in silos.

Secrecy is believed to give market participants an advantage over the rest of the field. This notion is particularly entrenched among players who see the markets as a zero sum game. Also, there is the general belief that algorithms and strategies lose their edge when used broadly.

We understand these concerns and accept they may have a root in reality. However, we believe there are alternative solutions that would render secrecy an optional feature, instead of a requirement in the system.

Our Approach

Break the Silos with a Global Crowd

A global crowd trains trading algorithms and puts them to compete. Once each competition ends, winners are open-sourced, improved and the new generation is put to compete again, unleashing a relentless evolutionary process that assures the prompt replacement of algos losing their edge.

Learn More

The project enables and incentivizes teams of developers, traders and analysts, both professionals and amateurs alike, to come together and train trading bots that compete on a wide array of algorithmic trading competitions.

Every time a competition finishes, bots' source code is opened within the community. The crowd pics the best performers and trains them for the next competition round, in the quest for fun, fame, glory and cash prizes.

The expected outcome is a relentless evolution of trading algorithms and a systematic increase in collective performance through each competition cycle.

After enough iterations, a wealth of strategies will have been explored and tested. In the process, substantial experience will have been accumulated, constructing the collective memory that will feed and train the superalgos of the future.

Trading Intelligence Marketplace

Democratizing Trading Technology

As intelligence grows, algorithms shall be put at the service of all people: investors of all calibers will be able to rent the crowd's trading intelligence to manage their assets. Rental fees will go directly to algo-makers and to cover operational expenses, with no intermediaries.

Algorithmic Trading World Championship

The Driver of Evolution

The Championship will be based on a multi-layer league system designed to drive evolution towards trading big capitals. Teams need to win minor leagues to move to higher leagues. As they progress, each league runs longer competitions with increasing capital and larger prize money.

Roadmap

The Path to Reorganizing Trading Intelligence

Minimum Viable Product: done!

The MVP was delivered in pre-alpha stage in October 2018, consisting of a platform enabling running bots in the cloud and on the browser, in live trading, competition and backtesting modes. It included running basic competitions, handling users, teams and API exchange keys.

It also included several bots, with a sensor connected to Poloniex, several indicators and a trader. The platform featured a visualization engine rendering bots' actions and datasets over a graphic timeline. Debugging of code and strategies over the timeline was done directly in the browser.

Alpha-stage Competitions: coming soon!

Testing-phase competitions start March 2019. Developers and traders can team up to train trading bots and put them to compete in week-long events measuring ROI as the metric to determine the winners.

The March 2019 event marks the start of the distribution of ALGO tokens within the community. One fourth of the total supply of tokens is destined to incentivizing competitions.

Algorithmic Trading World Championship

Superalgos is developing the system to run an elaborate multi-league tournament designed to promote evolution towards trading large capitals. The Championship thus, will be the enabler of the Marketplace.

One fundamental design goal of the league system is that teams winning a league get enough prize money to meet the capital requirements for the next league, promoting mobility across the ranks.

Trading Intelligence Marketplace

The marketplace is the business of the crowd creating and training algorithms. In the beginning, the crowd is incentivized solely by competition dynamics, cash prizes and ALGO tokens.

However, as soon as the collective trading intelligence becomes marketable, the crowds' intelligence shall be open to the public, constituting a direct line of business for algo-makers.
