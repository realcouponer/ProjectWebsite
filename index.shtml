The Status Quo

Trading Intelligence Evolves in Silos

  • Investment companies keep trading technology in absolute secrecy.

  • The assumption is made that this gives them an edge over other market participants.

  • It is also assumed that lose performance when used too broadly.

  • Even within a company, specialists work alone or in small groups.

  • Evolution of trading intelligence is therefore capped by the intelligence of small groups.

Learn More

We believe we have identified a systemic weakness in the reigning active asset management model; a weakness that may explain why no trading intelligence developed within investment firms has ever evolved to the point of dominating the space or achieving a remarkably superior performance in the markets.

Companies acquire significant talent in the form of data analysts and tech resources; however, in our view, they may be failing at organizing human and machine resources in a way that would yield the best possible results.

Sometimes due to incentives structures, workflows, organizational requirements or segmentation of incumbencies—save for a few exceptions—there is little collaboration within firms.

In fact, small teams and many times individuals work in absolute secrecy.

Needless to say, there certainly is zero cooperation in between firms.

Investment firms work in silos.

Secrecy is believed to give market participants an advantage over the rest of the field. This notion is particularly entrenched among players who see the markets as a zero sum game. Also, there is the general belief that and strategies lose their edge when used broadly.

We understand these concerns and accept they may have a root in reality. However, we believe there are alternative solutions that would render secrecy an optional feature, instead of a requirement in the system.

Our Approach

Challenge the Silos with a Global Open Collaboration

  • Algorithms participate in for prize money and reputation → shift in incentives!

  • After each Competition, Algorithms are open-sourced → collective knowledge levelled upwards!

The enables and incentivizes teams of developers, traders and analysts, both professionals and amateurs alike, to come together and train trading bots that compete on a wide array of .

Every time a competition finishes, bots' source code is opened within the . The crowd pics the best performers and trains them for the next competition round, in the quest for fun, fame, glory and cash prizes.

The expected outcome is a relentless evolution of trading algorithms and a systematic increase in collective performance through each competition cycle.

People are not forced to open source their work. Instead, people may keep any number of Algorithms secret, and only those used to compete become open source. Therefore, the Superalgos Project does not negate the current paradigm but improves it with a collaborative complement.

After enough iterations, a wealth of strategies will have been explored and tested. In the process, substantial experience will have been accumulated, constructing the collective memory that will feed and train the of the future.

A superalgo is not just a high-performance trading algorithm. It is a trading intelligence that will from within this evolutionary model.

The Path to Reorganizing Trading Intelligence

Project Kick-off: August 2017.

The stated with founder Luis Fernando Molina, a visionary entrepreneur, systems architect, with a track record in fintech developing mission-critical systems for banks. Together with his brother Julian, they set up a Core Team that helped deliver an MVP by October 2018.

The story of the inception of the project was documented by the brothers in a series of two articles published in Hacker Noon: Superalgos & The Trading Singularity, and Superalgos: Building a Trading Supermind.

Minimum Viable Product: Delivered, October 2018.

The MVP was delivered in pre-alpha stage in October 2018, consisting of a platform enabling running bots in the cloud and on the browser, in live trading, competition and backtesting modes. It included running basic competitions, handling users, teams and API exchange keys.

It also included several bots, with a sensor connected to Poloniex, several indicators and a trader. The platform featured a visualization engine rendering bots' actions and datasets over a graphic timeline. Debugging of code and strategies over the timeline was done directly in the browser.

Strategizer: Demo version coming soon!

We are very close to launching an alpha-stage demo version of the Strategizer, a tool that enables traders with little programming skills to define and automate complex strategies.

The tool offers pre-programmed strategies configurable via a GUI. Users may see the results in (almost) real time through a built-in simulation engine with historical and head-of-the-market data.

We expect the user experience to evolve until no coding skills whatsoever are required.

In addition, the Strategizer supports plugging in any number of strategies that programmers may develop, and enable the GUI-based configuration of those strategies too.

In the long term, the Strategizer will evolve into a complete suite of configurable strategies working under the coordination of a Central Command Algorithm. Strategies in the suite will cover all sorts of market conditions, and will implement risk management and capital allocation criterion to automate the management of assets.

Superalgos Network: Alpha version ETA Q3 2019.

The Superalgos Network is a P2P Network consisting of running both part of the and .

The main purpose of the Superalgos Network is to remove trust requirements by allowing all stakeholders to run their own instances of the Superalgos Platform.

Anyone may download the Superalgos Node and deploy it in their own server, becoming a Node Operator.

Node Operators may run the software for their own use or offer a hosted solution for users who chose to trust the operator (with their Algorithms' code and / or Exchange API Keys).

Superalgos Nodes work mostly on a stand-alone, independent basis in terms of their core activities, but may speak with each other to consume data and services from other nodes, and to relay relevant network information.

The data and services offered among nodes may be black-boxed (offered on an input / output basis, without revealing how the output is obtained) and sold for micro fees, payable in in between Algorithms. The transactions may include indicator data, signals or any form of trading intelligence that lives distributed across the network.

Exchanges shall give back discounts on transaction fees charged on the network's bundled trading volume, constituting a significant incentive to run Algorithms within the network instead of running them otherwise.
