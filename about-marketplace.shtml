Trading Intelligence Marketplace

The long-term vision of the Superalgos Project is a Marketplace running as a distributed system on the Superalgos Network—the gateway for the Community to access capital markets, attracting traders, investors, and asset managers.

In general terms, investors subscribe to the services of asset managers, who in turn hire the trading intelligence available in the network.

Asset managers are companies or individuals in the network and their services may vary in terms of sophistication according to the size of capitals they manage. Asset management services may be as elaborate as available elsewhere in the industry or as simple as an application connecting the subscription directly to Algorithms.

The network offers both open-source trading intelligence evolved within Competitions, but also offers close-source intelligence black-boxed within Algo-Maker Teams' services, and kept private from the rest of the Community.

As such, the Trading Intelligence Marketplace does not negate the existing model in which trading technology is developed in secret. Instead, it challenges proprietary intelligence with an open, collective alternative that—due to its built-in evolutionary model—may eventually perform better than the closed solutions.

Both closed, proprietary intelligence and open, collective intelligence services compete on a free-market basis. Algo-Makers, asset managers and investors are free to source the intelligence they need from anywhere in the Superalgos Network.

3. Investors use Intelli to pay for Asset Managers' subscription services.

The Algorithm obtaining the subscription autonomously pays for all the services it consumes, such as:

  • Node Operators, who offer execution and data storage services to Algorithms;

At the same time, Algorithms pay :

  • in the case of open-source Algorithms, the revenue is distributed based on a pro-rata of contributions in creating and training the respective Algorithm;

  • In the case of closed-source, the revenue goes to the sole proprietor of the Algorithm.
